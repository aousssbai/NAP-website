Problem and Solution


Why our project was created?
Why a need for a chatbot?

Context:

  • 6 million women shop and browse pages of the site each month
  • A highly engaged loyal audience with 85 million monthly page views
  • 3.4 million followers on social media

As YOOX Net-a-Porter Group has such diverse and extensive business areas, our client and our team will focus only on Net-a-Porter.com, which provides online retail services targetted at women.

With the focus of providing the best online shopping experience, it is vital to answer customersâ€™ queries in a timely manner. This will keep customers engaged on the website and they are more likely to make purchases.

Currently, Net-a-Porter has an 3rd-party assistance chat where customers can ask questions. However, it is an outsourced function, which means that the company has very little control over what information is conversed at the instant.

Moreover, the assistance chat is not functioning 24/7 and only sometimes appears on the website as it is operating by real people. The point is that a query not answered might result in the loss of a potential customer.


With our Customer Service Chatbot employing Natural Language Processing, Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning, it will be able to answer most of the customersâ€™ queries.

It will act as a filter between the customers and human fashion advisors, who are on-site Net-a-Porter employees.

This will allow Net-a-Porter to better control and customise the customersâ€™ experience on the website and analyse available query data to develop new business insights.

The Product-Focused

They know exactly what they want.

They have done the preparatory research and maybe just want to replace something they already have.

They might even have seen the product in store and just want to buy it online.

So they might not even look the product description and instead simply check the name is identical before confirming the order.

The main criteria is speed. They want to quickly locate the product on the website and purchase it.

To address these customers, the chatbot needs to aim for clear product identification with highly descriptive pictures.

The search has to be quick and relevant.

The chatbot needs to provide an easy access to previous orders in order to buy the same product again should the customer wish to replace it.

The checkout process must be fast and straightforward.

Alicia

The Leisure Shopper

They are leisure shoppers who go on the website for inspiration or just to kill time.

They are attached to the latest trends.

They look online prior to going to the store.

Their potential is the conversion from browser to buyer.

The most important aspect is to have visual and informative website, by showing them the latest trends, flash sales and the most bought items.

To address them, the chatbot has to:

- Recommend new and popular products.
- Provide easy access to recommended products and new inventory.
- Integrate with social platforms to share information about products they like.

Beatrice

The Bargain Hunter

They look for the best deals possible.

They want to locate the deals easily and quickly.

If they are satisfied with your deal, they might become repeat customers and recommend the website to their friends.

To address them, the chatbot has to:

- Display sales items alongside with full prices items, plus providing a full section of discounted items.
- List the product prices, associated with discounts and savings.
- Allow easy coupon redemption or applying discounts automatically when the criteria are met.

Cassandra

The One-Time Shopper

They do not have a specific customer profile and could be any of the 3 types described previously.

They are often giftcard users.

They might come for the first time and are not familiar with the site.

The navigation must be instinctive and user-friendly.

Product description must be complete.

But the most important aspect is to allow the customer to buy without any registration, which can be a laborious process, especially for the one-time shopper.

Dorothy

Testing



Testing Strategies for PoC



As our customer service chatbot is heavily focused on providing the best user experience, the accuracy and reliability of our query responses must be top-notch. Thus, it is crucial that we employ unit testing, integration testing and acceptance testing. All the frameworks and libraries described below are automated testing tools.



Unit Testing



The majority of our test suites will be made up of unit tests. Unit tests test for small units of code in insolation and ensure that each unit of code fulfils its intended purpose.

There are 2 main frameworks for Node.js unit testing, which are Mocha and Jasmine. The key difference is that Mocha does not have a built-in assertion library, whereas Jasmine contains one by default. Mocha is preferred as it allows for a choice in assertion libraries, such as should.js, Chai, expect.js and better-assert. Chai is a preferred assertion library as it again has the flexibility to use 3 assertion styles (should, expect and assert). It is also able to include 3rd party testing plugins such as sinonJS, which provides test spies, stubs and mocks to cover a wider scope of possible conditions. The flexibility from Mocha and Chai gives our team room to fully explore our options.

Integration Testing



Integration testing test the integrations between different units, for example the interaction between one module and its dependencies. Tests will be added and used regularly in form of API-level integration tests to verify the response of our API calls. Integration testing can be done through Mocha framework and Chai assertion library, or using CodeceptJS, where the former will be for development usage and the latter will be for client feedback.



Acceptance Testing



This is a validation activity where we evaluate our chatbot and determine if we have built the product that our client really needs. This form of testing will be done with the presence of the client, where we will run the functional tests and ensure that while we have fulfilled the requirements, it will also cover the scenarios that our client expects from the usage of the chatbot.

A popular acceptance testing framework is CodeceptJS, which is based on the Mocha testing framework. It specialises in test scenarios and product validation. The 3 key features is that it is scenario driven, backend agnostic and has an interactive shell. We can control the chatbot in real time by clicking buttons and providing input inside a running test. Acceptance tests are written from the userâ€™s perspective and each command is described as an action of a user using the chatbot, which is perfect for our purposes.

Our team will dive straight into development of our PoC once term 2 starts. We have weighed the pros and cons of available technologies. In the end, we have decided to use the IBM Watsonâ€™s suite of products, mainly Watson Conversation, as the core development tools to build our customer service chatbot. The backend programming language that we will use to process the user intents and generate the response is Node.js. We chose Node.js, over Java and Python, because all of our team members wanted to further expand our knowledge and experience in Node.js as it is currently a very popular programming language.

We have also experimented on using the IBM Watson technologies. We have created an account on IBM Bluemix and have began to familiarise ourselves with the development environment in Watson Conversation by creating a test workspace and trying out the intents and entities component. However, we have not been able to begin development in term 1 as we have to review the API documentation with our client, and it impacted our progress as all of our query responses will be composed with the API return information. Overall, we have not met with any significant problems in our usage of the technologies. Our team is optimistic in successfully delivering our intended customer service chatbot that fulfils the core requirements.
