Problem and Solution





Why our project was created? Why a need for a chatbot?

As YOOX Net-a-Porter Group has such diverse and extensive business areas, our client and our team will focus only on Net-a-Porter.com, which provides online retail services targetted at women.

With the focus of providing the best online shopping experience, it is vital to answer customers’ queries in a timely manner. This will keep customers engaged on the website and they are more likely to make purchases.

Currently, Net-a-Porter has an 3rd-party assistance chat where customers can ask questions. However, it is an outsourced function, which means that the company has very little control over what information is conversed at the instant.

Moreover, the assistance chat is not functioning 24/7 and only sometimes appears on the website as it is operating by real people. The point is that a query not answered might result in the loss of a potential customer.







6 Million

6 million women shop and browse pages of the site each month


85 Million

A highly engaged loyal audience with 85 million monthly page views


3.4 Million

3.4 million followers on social media





With our Customer Service Chatbot employing Natural Language Processing, Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning, it will be able to answer most of the customers’ queries.

It will act as a filter between the customers and human fashion advisors, who are on-site Net-a-Porter employees.

This will allow Net-a-Porter to better control and customise the customers’ experience on the website and analyse available query data to develop new business insights.

Personas and Use Cases





The product-Focused

They know exactly what they want

They have done the preparatory research and maybe just want to replace something they already have.

They might even have seen the product in store and just want to buy it online.

So they don’t even look the product description, just check the name is the same and confirm the order.

The main purpose is speed. They just want to locate the product on the website and purchase it.

To catch these customers, the chatbot needs to aim for a clear product identification, with very descriptive pictures.

The search has to be quick and relevant.

The chatbot needs to provide an easy access to previous orders in order to buy the same product again if the customer wants to replace it The checkout process must be fast and straightforward.

The browsers

They are leisurely shoppers who go on the website for inspiration or just to kill time.

They are attached to the latest trends.

They look online prior to going to the store.

Their potential is the conversion from browser to buyer.

The most important aspect is to have great website, by showing them the latest trends and interesting sales, the most bought items To catch them, the chatbot has to :

- List new, popular.

- Easy access to recommended products and new inventory.

- Ability to share information about products they like.

The bargain hunters

They look for the best deal possible.

They want to locate the deals easily and very fast.

If they are satisfied with your deal, they might become repeat customers Important features of the chatbot:

- Display sales items alongside with full prices items, plus providing a full section of discounted items.

- List the product prices, associated with discounts and savings.

- Allow easy coupon redemption or applying discounts automatically when criteria are met.

The one-time shoppers

They do not have a specific customer profile and could be any of the 4 types described previously.

They are often giftcard users.

They might come for the first time and are not familiar with the site.

The navigation must be instinctive and very clear.

Product description must be complete.

But the most important aspect is to allow the customer to buy without any registration, which can be a laborious process, especially for those one-time shoppers.
